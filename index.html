About Me

Ganling Zhou is a New York-based Sound Supervisor, Composer, and Creative Technologist.

An alumnus of New York University’s (NYU) Music Technology program, Zhou represents the next generation of hybrid film and media creatives. They currently serve as a resident technologist and gallery manager at Harvestworks, New York City's premier contemporary media art center. Backed by an elite institutional pedigree from NYU and advanced research frameworks from IRCAM (Institute for Research and Coordination in Acoustics/Music, France), Zhou seamlessly bridges the gap between complex software architecture and visceral sonic artistry.

As a rare "dual-threat" creator proficient in both algorithmic systems and physical computing, Zhou specializes in advanced digital signal processing (DSP), real-time communications, and intricate spatial audio frameworks. Rejecting conventional scoring tropes, they treat film audio as a living, breathing architectural space. By blending raw instrumental expression with industrial physical acoustics and avant-garde synthesis, Zhou constructs high-gravity, deeply immersive sonic universes—offering cinema and multimedia installations an elevated artistic edge and a world-class, festival-ready identity.

Their original sonic installations and hardware-integrated soundscapes have been deployed and exhibited at major creative landmarks, including the Yale Center for Collaborative Arts and Media (CCAM) and Governors Island.
